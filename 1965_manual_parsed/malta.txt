MALTA, G.C.



Following the Malta Independence Conference held in London from the 16th July 1963 to the 1st August 1963, under the chairmanship of the Secretary of State for the Colonies, and subsequent constitutional discussions held in London during the remainder of that year and in 1964, Malta attained independence and became a member of the Commonwealth on the 21st September 1964.



The Secretary of State for Commonwealth Relations is now the channel of communication between Her Majesty’s Government in the United Kingdom and Her Majesty’s Government in Malta.



Basic information about Malta can be found in the 1964 edition of the Colonial Office List.
