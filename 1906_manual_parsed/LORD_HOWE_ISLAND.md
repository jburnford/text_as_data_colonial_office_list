LORD HOWE ISLAND.

Lord Howe Island is a dependency of New South Wales, about six miles long, and one mile broad. Its population at the census of 1901 was males 68, females 52, total 120. It is a mountainous, well-wooded island. The land in some places is very rich, and remarkable for its equable and delightful climate. It is situated about 150 miles E. of Sydney, in 31° 30' S. lat. and 159° 25' E. long., and forms part of the State, being administered by a visiting magistrate from Sydney.

The islet of Ball's Pyramid lies between Lord Howe and Norfolk Islands.

Visiting Magistrate, Frank Farnell, J.P.
