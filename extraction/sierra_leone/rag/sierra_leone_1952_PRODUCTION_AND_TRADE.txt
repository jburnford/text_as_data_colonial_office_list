PRODUCTION AND TRADE

The total value of imports in 1950 was £6,745,865 and of exports, £6,959,130. In these figures alloy and nickel specie and currency notes are included.
at intrinsic value and exports do not include the difference between receipts and payments of the Sierra Leone Produce Marketing Board.

The principal import was cotton piece goods valued at £1,579,000. Other important imports were: wearing apparel, machinery, coal, tobacco, flour, and sugar. The largest export was palm kernels valued at £2,278,000; other important exports being diamonds, iron ore, ginger, piassava, cocoa, kola nuts, palm oil, groundnuts and chrome ore.
