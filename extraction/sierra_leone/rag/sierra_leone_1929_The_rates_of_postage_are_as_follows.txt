The rates of postage are as follows:

| Letters, Post Cards | Newspapers, Books, etc., per 2 ozs. |
|---------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Internal            |                                   |
| To U.K. British     | 2 per oz.                         |
| Dominions and Colonies additional oz.) |                                   |
| To Other Countries  | 3 ½                               |

Parcels: maximum weight 22 lbs. for places inland and to certain countries which accept heavy parcels; 11 lbs. other countries.

Inland 6d. for 2 lbs.; 3d. each additional lb.

U.K., W.A. Colonies, Liberia—Up to 3 lbs., 2s.; 7 lbs., 3s.; 11 lbs., 4s.

Parcels for other countries are forwarded through the U.K.

British Imperial Postal Orders are issued and paid in the Colony; there is also an Inland and Foreign Money Order Service.

At the close of 1927 the Post Office Savings Bank held £64,030 to the credit of 6,410 depositors.
