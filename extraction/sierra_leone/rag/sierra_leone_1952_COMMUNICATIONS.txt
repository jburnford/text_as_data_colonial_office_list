COMMUNICATIONS

Railway

The total revenue and expenditure of the Sierra Leone Government Railway for 1950 was £447,312 and £545,126 respectively.

Shipping

The tonnage of shipping entered and cleared in 1950 was as under:

| British Tonnage | Total Tonnage |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Entered         | 1,852,835     |
| Cleared         | 1,851,357     |

Air

The landing strips at Bonthe, Kambia and Waterloo are no longer in use.

Posts and Telegraphs

There are now about 2,000 miles of trunk telephone and telegraph lines in the territory and about 2,000 miles of local and urban telephone lines in the Colony and other centres. The trunk system now includes Bo, Mano, Freetown, Njala, Moyamba, Sembehun and Waterloo. On 1st January, 1951, the Post and Telegraphs Department took over responsibility for the telegraph service from the Sierra Leone Government Railway.
