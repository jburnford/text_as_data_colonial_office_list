### Marketing and Co-operative Organizations

The Sierra Leone Produce Marketing Board was set up under Ordinance No. 1 of 1949, the objects being to secure the most favourable arrangements for the purchase, marketing and export of Sierra Leone produce, and to assist, by all possible means, in the development of the agricultural industry in Sierra Leone and of secondary industries connected with it. To this end the Board has erected six pioneer mills for the extraction and processing of palm oil and two are in course of erection.

On 30th April, 1954, there were 135 Co-operative Societies, with 5,643 members and subscribed capital of £4,454. Their surplus and reserves totalled £6,328. Their turnover during the year ending on that date was £89,000. Government has authorized the advance of up to £20,000 in loans to co-operative societies, mostly to assist them in giving short-term loans to their members.
