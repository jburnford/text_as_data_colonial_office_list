Means of Communication.

The Liverpool mail steamers call every three weeks, and there is also regular communication via Dakar with Bordeaux. Four telegraph cables place Bathurst in communication with Europe and West Africa. There are no railways, internal telegraphs or metalled roads, but horses will live, and the river forms an unrivalled highway for 300 miles. The postage of letters to Europe, United States, and West Africa, is 4d. per. ½ oz. There is no internal post.
