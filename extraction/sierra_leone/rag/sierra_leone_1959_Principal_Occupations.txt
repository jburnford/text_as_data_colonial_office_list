Colony: Sierra Leone
Year: 1959
Section: Principal Occupations
Type: narrative
---

It is estimated that over half the population is engaged in agriculture. Returns from employers of six or more workers show the numbers employed in the main industrial groups at the end of 1957 to be as follows:

| Industry                        | Number |
|--------------------------------|--------|
| Building and Construction      | 9,784   |
| Mining                         | 6,908   |
| Transport (road, rail and air) | 7,271   |
| Maritime and Waterfront        | 2,999   |
| Commerce                       | 5,740   |
| Other principal industries and services (including public administration, health and agriculture) | 15,919 |

These workers served the following employers: Government 25,156; Native administrations 2,443; Service Departments (civilian employees of War Department and Admiralty) 1,214; and major private and commercial undertakings 19,808.

In addition an estimated 32,500 workers were employed by smaller employers i.e. of less than six persons. During 1957 the average monthly figure of all workers in wage-earning employment was approximately 78,000.

There were, at the end of 1957, 12 registered trade unions; eleven of these, including one employer's union with 18 members, had a total membership of 28,466. The membership figure of the twelfth union is not available.
