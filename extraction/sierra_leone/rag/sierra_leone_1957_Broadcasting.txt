Colony: Sierra Leone
Year: 1957
Section: Broadcasting
Type: narrative
---

The Sierra Leone Broadcasting Service, transmitting from Freetown, was inaugurated on the 19th October, 1955 and at present operates between the hours of 17·50 and 20·00 on a frequency of 3,316 kilocycles in the 90 metre band with a power of 5 kilowatts. The Service consists of approximately two hours' local programme and the remainder re-radiated B.B.C. Overseas Service. It is planned to extend the hours of direct broadcasting early in 1957. On the 1st October, 1956, the number of licences issued to owners of private sets was 2,048.

Government also operates a wired system in Freetown which broadcasts local programmes and the B.B.C. Overseas Service. On the 1st October, 1956, the number of subscribers was 3,896.

The operation and maintenance of both systems are in charge of the Posts and Telegraphs Department, whilst programmes are arranged by the Public Relations Department.
