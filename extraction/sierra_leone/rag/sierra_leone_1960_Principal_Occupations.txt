Principal Occupations

It is estimated that over half the population is engaged in agriculture. Returns from employers of six or more workers show the numbers employed in the main industrial groups at the end of 1958 to be as follows:

| Industry                        | Number |
|--------------------------------|--------|
| Building and Construction       | 7,556  |
| Mining                         | 6,596  |
| Transport (road, rail and air)  | 6,964  |
| Maritime and Waterfront         | 2,048  |
| Commerce                       | 5,568  |
| Public Administration Offices   | 5,666  |
| Others                         | 9,931  |

These workers served the following employers: Government 23,834; Native administrations
In addition an estimated 34,000 workers were employed by smaller employers i.e. of less than six persons. During 1958 the average monthly figure of all workers in wage-earning employment was approximately 79,000.

There were, at the end of 1958, 14 registered trade unions; eleven of these, including one employer's union with 18 members, had a total membership of 27,606. The membership figure of the remaining three unions, including two employers' unions, is not available.
