Year: 1954
Section: The chief minerals mined are iron-ore, diamonds and chrome ore.
Type: narrative

The chief minerals mined are iron-ore, diamonds and chrome ore. Small quantities of gold are also found. Production in 1952 was:

- Iron ore . . . . . . 1,164,441 tons
- Diamond . . . . . . 452,618 carats
- Chromite . . . . . . 23,493 tons
- Gold . . . . . . 2,892 ozs.

Other minerals known to occur in Sierra Leone are platinum, ilmenorutile, lignite, cassiterite and molybdenite.

Six pioneer oil mills for the expressing of palm oil are operated by the Sierra Leone Produce Marketing Board. An oil-expressing plant for the production of groundnut and kernel oil is expected to be in operation by early 1954. It will be operated by Government. A hammer-mill will be incorporated in the plant to produce...
pig feed. Government operates one small and two large rice mills and there are a few privately owned mills, all on a small scale. Furniture making is carried on by Government and private enterprise. A privately owned soap factory produces approximately 100 tons of soap per year. Village industries include weaving, hand methods of expressing palm oil, and cracking of palm nuts to obtain the kernel. Mechanical coffee-hulling is also being introduced and experiments are being made with the mechanical preparation of foofoo.
