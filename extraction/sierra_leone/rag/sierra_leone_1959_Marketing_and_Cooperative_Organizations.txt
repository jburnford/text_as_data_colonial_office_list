### Marketing and Co-operative Organizations

The Sierra Leone Produce Marketing Board was set up under Ordinance No. 1 of 1949, the objects being to secure the most favourable arrangements for the purchase, marketing and export of Sierra Leone produce, and to assist, by all possible means, in the development of the agricultural industry in Sierra Leone and of secondary industries connected with it.

On 31st December, 1957, there were 313 co-operative societies with 15,000 members, and with their capital and savings amounting to over £29,000. Their surplus and reserves were over £26,000. Their turnover during the year ending on that date was £277,700.

The most important items were cocoa and coffee marketing which amounted to over £135,000. Rice marketing developed considerably to a value of over £61,400. An important new work was the undertaking of mechanical rice cultivation by means of equipment co-operatively owned. Twenty-two tractors with ancillary equipment cultivated successfully over 3,500 acres.
