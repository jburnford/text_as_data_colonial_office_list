PRODUCTION AND TRADE

The total value of imports into Sierra Leone in 1948 was £4,979,350 and of exports £4,164,566. Corresponding figures for 1947 were £4,586,922 and £2,801,727. As in previous years the export figures do not include the value of diamonds. If this figure, and the value of produce differentials were added to the export values, the apparent adverse balance would be turned into a very favourable balance of visible trade.

The direction of trade in 1948 was exports 70·86 per cent. to the United Kingdom as compared with 77·05 in 1947; 21·99 per cent. to other British possessions, as compared with 14·87 in 1947; and 2·63 per cent. to United States of America as compared with 6·57 in 1947: Imports — 64·88 per cent. from United Kingdom as compared with 46·54 in 1947, 17·79 from other British possessions as compared with 27·43 in 1947, 4·21 from United States of America as compared with 13·86 the previous year.

The largest single import in 1948 was cotton piece goods valued at £1,128,702. This figure when compared with the 1947 figure of £1,019,000 shows an increase of 10·7 per cent. If however the quantities imported in both years, i.e. yardage, are compared the increase in 1948 is only 3·0 per cent.

The next largest import was coal valued at £337,585. Other large imports were unmanufactured tobacco; bags and sacks (empty), not including paper bags; and all kinds of electrical and telegraphic apparatus and telephone apparatus.

The largest single export in 1948 was palm kernels valued at £1,744,591. Compared with 1947, this figure shows an increase of 34 per cent. When quantities are compared however the increase in 1948 is only 8·6 per cent. on the 1947 figure. Next in value were diamonds, iron ore and kola nuts. There are certain export commodities which showed marked increases on the 1947 figures. In 1947 the export of bennised fell from £1,949 in 1946 to £55 but in 1948 the figure rose to £11,538. Cocoa increased from £16,930 in 1947 to £102,941 in 1948. The export of palm oil is steadily improving. From £30,857 in 1947 the figure rose to £116,364 in 1948.

Shipment of wild oil beans (pentaclertha) was not up to expectation the chief reason being that the Ministry of Food was not enthusiastic over the purchase of small quantities.

The experiment in trawler fishing carried out by the Government in 1947 was a success. It is hoped that with the arrival shortly of a new trawler the Government will be able to do much constructive work in sea-fishing, and produce additional supplies of fish for the Protectorate where it is badly needed.

Domestic requirements of sawn timber were met by the two Forest Department sawmills: primary production in 1948 amounted to 233,740 cubic feet of sawn timber, and 245,000 shingles. Woodwork machine-shops were also put into operation during the year by the Forest Department: sales of furniture and joinery totalled £1,913 in value.
