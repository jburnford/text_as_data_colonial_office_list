Currency and Banking.

Besides English currency, 5 franc pieces of the Latin union are legal tender, and West Africa silver coins (2s., 1s., 6d. and 3d.) were put into circulation in 1913. Currency Notes of the value of 20s. and 10s. were introduced in 1916 and of 2s. in 1917. The Bank of British West Africa has two branches in the Colony and some agencies in the Protectorate. The Colonial Bank also is established in Freetown. A Government Savings Bank was established in 1882, and had invested by the end of 1917, the sum of 122,057l.
