Education.

The number of schools on the assisted list in 1927 was 130. Fourteen schools were maintained wholly from public funds, making a total of 144. Of these 130 were primary, 2 industrial and 11 secondary. At the end of 1927 the enrolment number was 14,386. Education is not compulsory. The Church Missionary Society and the Wesleyan Mission have a college at Fourah Bay affiliated to Durham University. A scheme for the education, on Western lines, of Mohammedan children was started in 1901. There are now five Mohammedan Schools or Madrasas in Freetown, with 695 pupils on the roll in 1927. A Government school for the sons and nominees of native chiefs was established at Bo in the Kenema District of the Protectorate on 1st March, 1906, under European supervision. The number of pupils at the close of the year 1927 was 159. There is also a Government Model School in Freetown at which 263 pupils attended in 1927.

An agricultural training college for vernacular teachers was established in 1919 at Njala in the Protectorate; 130 pupils were enrolled in 1927. There are also eight Government Rural schools in the Protectorate, with an attendance of 331 pupils.
