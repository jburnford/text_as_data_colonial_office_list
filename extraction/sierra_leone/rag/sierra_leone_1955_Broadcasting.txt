Broadcasting

Government operates a wired broadcasting system in Freetown which rediffuses the B.B.C. Overseas Service and local programmes to which there were 3,500 subscribers in August 1954. In the same month the number of licences issued to owners of private sets was 818. The operation and maintenance of the station is in charge of the Posts and Telegraphs Department while the rediffusion programmes are arranged by the Public Relations Department.

There is no direct broadcasting at present but it is expected that a 5 kW transmitter will be brought into operation in April, 1955.
