Education.

The number of schools on the assisted list in 1925 was 134. Sixteen schools were maintained wholly from public funds, making a total of 150. Of these 135 were elementary, 8 industrial and 7 secondary. At the end of 1925 the enrolment number was 12,960. Education is not compulsory. The Church Missionary Society and the Wesleyan Mission have a college at Fourah Bay affiliated to Durham University. A scheme for the education, on Western lines, of Mohammedan children was started in 1901. There are now five Mohammedan Schools or Madrasas in Freetown, with 726 pupils on the roll in 1925. A Government school for the sons and nominees of native chiefs was established at Bo in the Kenema District of the Protectorate on 1st March, 1906, under European supervision. The number of pupils at the close of the year 1925 was 161. There is also a Government Model School in Freetown at which 231 pupils attended in 1925.

An agricultural training college for vernacular teachers was established in 1919 at Njala in the Protectorate; 110 pupils were enrolled in 1925. There are also eight Government Rural schools in the Protectorate, with an attendance of 328 pupils.
