Marketing and Co-operative Organizations

The Sierra Leone Produce Marketing Board was set up under Ordinance No. 1 of 1949, the objects being to secure the most favourable arrangements for the purchase, marketing and export of Sierra Leone produce, and to assist, by all possible means, in the development of the agricultural industry in Sierra Leone and of secondary industries connected with it.

On 31st December, 1958, there were 328 co-operative societies with a membership of 20,000. Their turnover for the year was roughly £433,000.

The most important new development has been the formation of the South East Co-operative Federation Limited, which includes all cocoa and coffee marketing societies in the province, and its appointment as a buying agent of the Produce Marketing Board, starting work in September, 1958. All "co-operative" cocoa is being handled by it, the total at the end of the year being 40% of the country's cocoa for the season. The value of cocoa co-operatively marketed during the year has risen from £126,000 to £154,000.

The mechanized cultivation of rice has continued with some success. Rice marketing turnover has been increased to £107,000.

The amount guaranteed by Government to be operated through the Registrar's Loan Account has been increased to £200,000. Of this, some £60,000 is utilized to finance the cocoa-buying agency work in addition to a maximum of £50,000 made available by the banks as commercial produce advances. About £100,000 is being used for normal short term credit.
