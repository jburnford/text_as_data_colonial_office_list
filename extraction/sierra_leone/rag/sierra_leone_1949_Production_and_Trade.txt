**Production and Trade**

The total value of imports into Sierra Leone in 1947 was £4,586,922, and of exports £2,801,727. Corresponding figures in 1946 were £3,961,384 and £2,139,624. In both years the export figures exclude the value of exported diamonds which, if added, will considerably reduce the apparent adverse balance.

The direction of trade in 1947 was—exports 78·33 per cent. to the United Kingdom, 12·82 per cent. to other British possessions and 7·37 per cent. to the U.S.A. Imports 46·38 per cent. from the United Kingdom, 24·79 per cent. from other British possessions and 12·90 per cent. from the U.S.A.

The largest single import in 1947 was cotton piece goods valued at £1,019,000 with coal valued at £436,064 as the next largest import. Other large imports were tobacco, wearing apparel, provisions (especially flour), beer, spirits, wines, motor vehicles, motor fuel and oils, hardware, hollow-ware and building materials.

Palm kernels valued at £1,301,742 were the largest single export in 1947, with diamonds, iron ore and kola nuts next in value. Other exports of value were chrome ore, ginger, cocoa, piassava, palm oil, gold, platinum, gum copal and rubber.

An experimental shipment of 40 tons of wild oil beans (pentaclethra) was made as a result of representations to the Oil Seeds Commission, and it is hoped a small but valuable trade in this commodity may develop.

In the Colony Area farming is largely confined to production of cassava and garden crops such as maize and vegetables for the Freetown market. In the Protectorate the principal agricultural product is rice, which is the staple food of the country, with cassava as an important supplement to the diet. Rice is grown for local needs, which are estimated to be between 160,000 and 200,000 tons a year, but there is no regular surplus for export. Developments in local livestock production have been made by the establishment of a new Research Station at Musaia, and a marked expansion of the pig industry centred at Newton.
