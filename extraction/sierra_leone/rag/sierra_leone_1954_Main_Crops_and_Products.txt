Main Crops and Products

The main crops and products are palm kernels and palm oil, piassava, rice, cocoa, coffee, groundnuts, cassava, benniseed, Kola-nuts, bananas and ginger.

The livestock population (rough estimate) is: cattle 69,000, goats 21,000, sheep 10,000, pigs 4,000.

The main timber products in 1952 (figures in solid cubic feet) were:—sawn timber 366,000; shingles 7,000; roundwood 3,000,000; firewood 90,088,000; charcoal 10,000 and gum copal to the value of £822. Furniture to the value of £15,400 was made by the Government Forest Industries Branch. Sawn timber is not available for export but small quantities of gum copal and charcoal are exported.

Inshore fishing is extensive but insufficient to meet the demand in Freetown. Trawler fishing, operated by Government, has not proved successful and the venture has been closed down. The West African Fisheries Research Institute has a station at Kissy, near Freetown. Their research trawler Cape St. Mary is operated from this station. A series of mechanical failures has prevented a full fishing programme from being carried out up to the present time with the trawler. The Institute also possesses two seine boats, which are undertaking an intensive fishing and hydrological survey of the Sierra Leone River estuary.
