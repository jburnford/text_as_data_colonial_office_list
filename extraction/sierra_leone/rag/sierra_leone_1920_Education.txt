Education.

There are 144 elementary and intermediate mission schools in the Colony and Protectorate, of which 119 are assisted by the Government. At the end of 1918 the enrolment number was 8,481. There are 4 assisted Industrial or Trades Schools conducted by the Roman Catholic Mission; the total enrolment number was 200. Education is not compulsory. There are 10 secondary schools in the Colony, 3 of which are assisted by the Government, the total enrolment being 1,057. The Church Missionary Society has a college at Fourah Bay affiliated to Durham University. A scheme for the education, on Western lines, of Mohammedan children was started in 1901. There are now five Mohammedan Schools or Madrasas in Freetown supported by the Government, with 641 pupils on the roll in 1918. A Government school for the sons and nominees of native chiefs was established at Bo in the Railway District of the Protectorate on 1st March, 1906, under European supervision. The number of pupils at the close of the year 1918 was 130. There is also a Government Model School in Freetown at which over 300 pupils at present attend.

An agricultural training college for vernacular teachers has now been established at Njala in the Protectorate; 30 pupils have already been enrolled. There are also two Anglo-vernacular schools at Baiima and Gbangbama in the Rouietta and Northern Sherbro Districts respectively, with an attendance of 80 pupils.
