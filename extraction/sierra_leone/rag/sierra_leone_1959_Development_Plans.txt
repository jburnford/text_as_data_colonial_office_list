Development Plans

"An Outline of the Ten-Year Plan for the Development of Sierra Leone" was published in 1946 (and reviewed in 1949 when "A Plan of Economic Development for Sierra Leone" was published). The Plans provided for the development of Communications, Natural Resources and Social and Basic Services at a total cost of £5,000,000 in the ten-year period.

As contributions towards this expenditure, allocations of £2,600,000 and £1,200,000 under the Colonial Development and Welfare Acts of 1945 and 1955 were made.

The amount which it is expected to spend on development (largely on works construction) in the period 1st January 1958 to 31st December 1960 is £11,530,576 made up as follows:

- Lungi Airport: £90,896
- Inland Waterways: £40,000
- Freetown Electricity Supply: £446,029
- Provincial Electricity Supplies: £99,856
- Provincial Water Supplies: £323,214
- Roads and Bridges: £2,493,601
- Railway and Port Development: £2,005,770
- Guma Valley Hydro-electric Scheme: £1,463,209
- Agriculture: £716,276
- Schools and Colleges: £1,557,213
- Hospitals and Clinics: £298,516
- Public Buildings and Housing Schemes: £1,860,040
- Fisheries Research: £43,556
- Forest Industries: £43,000
- Broadcasting: £45,000
- Geological Survey: £4,400

Total: £11,530,576

It is hoped to finance this programme from loan funds, the Sierra Leone Development and Education Funds, and from the territory's allocations under the 1945 and 1955 Colonial Development and Welfare Acts.
