Main Crops and Products

The main crops and products are palm kernels and palm oil, piassava, rice, cocoa, coffee, groundnuts, cassava, bennised, Kola-nuts, bananas and ginger.

The livestock population (rough estimate) is: cattle 150,000, goats 25,000, sheep 10,000, pigs 4,000.

The main timber products in 1953 (figures in solid cubic feet) were:—sawn timber 365,000; shingles 4,000; roundwood 3,000,000; firewood 90,015,000; charcoal 14,000 and gum copal to the value of £600. Furniture to the value of £18,502 was made by the Government Forest Industries Branch. Sawn timber is not available for export but small quantities of gum copal and charcoal are exported.

Inshore fishing by African fishermen is extensive but insufficient to meet the demand in Freetown and the Protectorate. One British and one Italian trawler have recently begun operations from Freetown.
The Headquarters of the West African Fisheries Research Institute are situated at Kissy, near Freetown. Their research trawler has been operating from Takoradi in the Gold Coast during the months April-July 1954, and their launches have maintained an intensive inshore research programme throughout the year. The Institute also possesses two seine boats, which are undertaking an intensive fishing and hydrological survey of the Sierra Leone River estuary.

The chief minerals mined are iron-ore, diamonds and chrome ore. Small quantities of gold are also found. Production in 1953 was:

- Iron ore . . . . . . 1,367,986 tons
- Diamond . . . . . . 481,692 carats
- Chromite . . . . . . 24,334 tons
- Gold . . . . . . . . 1,645 ozs.

Other minerals known to occur in Sierra Leone are platinum, ilmenorutile, lignite, cassiterite and molybdenite.

Eight pioneer oil mills for the expressing of palm oil are operated by the Sierra Leone Produce Marketing Board. An oil-expressing plant for the production of groundnut and kernel oil is expected to be in operation in 1955. It will be operated by Government. A hammermill will be incorporated in the plant to produce pig feed. Government operates one small and two large rice mills and there are a few privately owned mills, all on a small scale. Furniture making is carried on by Government and private enterprise. Two privately owned soap factories produce approximately 125 tons of soap per year. Village industries include weaving, hand methods of expressing palm oil, and cracking of palm nuts to obtain the kernel. Mechanical coffee-hulling is also being introduced and experiments are being made with the mechanical preparation of foofoo. A mobile, power-driven, palm nut cracking machine has been developed and tested and is being gradually introduced in the country. Increasing assistance continued to be given to the banana industry throughout the year.
