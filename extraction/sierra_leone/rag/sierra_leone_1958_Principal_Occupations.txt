Principal Occupations

It is estimated that over half the population is engaged in agriculture. Returns from employers of six or more workers show the numbers employed in the main industrial groups at the end of 1956 to be as follows:

- Building and Construction: 8,157
- Mining: 5,897
- Transport (road, rail and air): 5,808
- Maritime and Waterfront: 3,017
- Commerce: 5,569
- Other principal industries and services (including public administration, health and agriculture): 16,401

These workers served the following employers:

- Government: 16,598
- Native administrations: 2,613
- Service Departments (civilian employees of War Department and Admiralty): 1,709
- Major private and commercial undertakings: 23,789

In addition an estimated 32,500 workers were employed by smaller employers i.e. of less than six persons. During 1956 the average monthly figure of all workers in wage-earning employment was approximately 77,331.

There were, at the end of 1956, 11 registered trade unions with a total membership of 26,085, including one employers' union with 22 members.
