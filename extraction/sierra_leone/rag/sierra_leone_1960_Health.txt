Health

Diseases contended with in Sierra Leone, in addition to such infections as typhoid and dysentery, which are common, include malaria, schistosomiasis, leprosy, tuberculosis, goitre, rabies, yellow fever, trypanosomiasis, smallpox, cerebro-spinal fever and yaws. The incidence of the last five is comparatively low. The Health Service includes an Endemic Diseases Control Unit, an Entomological Laboratory and the former Malaria Control Unit.

In 1958 beds available in hospitals (including two Infirmarys and a maternity hospital) totalled 1,551 (Government 1,173, Missions 284, Mining 94). In-patients numbered 20,285 and out-patients 257,559. There were 39 Government dispensaries and Health Centres (excluding 24 treatment centres attached to the Endemic Diseases Control Unit) at which 132,134 new cases were treated.

Actual expenditure on medical and allied services in 1958 was £1,023,721. By comparison actual expenditure in 1935 was £63,923.
