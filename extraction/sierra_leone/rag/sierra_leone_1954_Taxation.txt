Taxation

There are three forms of direct taxation in force: income tax, poll tax, and house tax. Income tax was chargeable on individuals in 1952-53 at graduated rates (beginning at 6d. in the £) subject to personal reliefs and other deductions. An unmarried man was granted £300, and a married man £500 as an initial relief. Companies paid a flat rate of 9s. in the £ and a double taxation agreement has been concluded with Her Majesty's Government. Poll tax is payable by non-natives at the rate of £4 a year and is offset against an individual's liability for income tax. A house tax of 5s. a year is payable in the Protectorate by all adult native males, and a Settler's Fee of £1 by non-natives.

The two main forms of indirect taxation are the trade tariffs and excise duties.
