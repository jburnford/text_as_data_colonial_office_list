The amount which it is expected to spend on development (largely on works construction) in the period 1st January 1957 to 31st December 1959 is £9,985,502 made up as follows:

- Lungi Airport £40,000
- Inland Waterways £13,500
- Freetown Electricity Supplies £401,718
- Provincial Electricity Supplies £103,204
- Provincial Water Supplies £174,558
- Roads and Bridges £1,634,214
- Railway and Port Development £1,464,457
- Guma Valley Hydro-electric Scheme £2,683,411
- Agriculture £778,509
- Schools and Colleges £1,282,281
- Hospitals and Clinics £220,191
- Public Buildings and Housing Schemes £1,171,000
- Fisheries Research £18,459

£9,985,502

It is hoped to finance this programme from loan funds, the Development and Education Funds, and from the territory's allocations under the 1945 and 1955 Colonial Development and Welfare Acts. The allocation under the 1955 Act is £1,200,000.
