Education

University courses in Divinity, Arts, Commerce and Education are provided at Fourah Bay College (University of Durham), Freetown. At the beginning of 1952-53 session, 155 students were in attendance at these courses. Forty scholarships for higher education were awarded in 1952 (10 for study overseas, 10 for degree courses at Fourah Bay College, and 20 for Higher School Certificate and General Certificate of Education courses at Fourah Bay College, Prince of Wales School and Bo Government School). Forty-seven Sierra Leone scholarship students and about 260 private students were known to be studying in the United Kingdom. About 40 private students were known to be studying in the U.S.A.

There are four Teacher Training Colleges and two Teacher Training Departments in the territory; 407 students, including 20 agricultural instructors, were in training in 1952.

There are two Government Secondary Grammar Schools, three Government Secondary Central Schools and nine assisted Secondary Schools in the territory; 2,865 students were enrolled in 1952. There are also two unassisted Secondary Schools, which had 261 pupils. Four Government, 203 assisted and 107 unassisted Primary Schools had an enrolment of 39,216 pupils.

Literacy campaigns were conducted by the Protectorate Literature Bureau in 41 chiefdoms and 961 reading certificates were issued. Four thousand people became literate in a vernacular and 26,565 books in the vernacular were sold in 1952.

The Extramural Department of Fourah Bay College, the British Council and the Social Welfare Department organized adult education courses in various subjects.

Expenditure from all sources on education was approximately £625,354 in 1952 of which £92,265 was contributed from Colonial Development and Welfare funds and £43,952 by the Missions. In 1935 expenditure amounted to £38,518.
