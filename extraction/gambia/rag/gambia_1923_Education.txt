**Education.**

The Christian schools, which are wholly in the hands of the several denominations, receive grants-in-aid. Of ordinary elementary schools in Bathurst, there are one Anglican, two Wesleyan and two Roman Catholic, with 1,408 scholars in all. Education is not compulsory, and fees are charged. There is also a Wesleyan High school for boys under European control, with 60 pupils. There is also a Mohammedan school with 200 pupils, at which the ordinary rudiments of an English and Arabic education are taught, the upkeep of the premises and the payment of the teachers being borne by the Government, the native Mohammedan community contributing a small fixed annual sum. At MacCarthy Island there is an elementary Wesleyan school with 84 pupils, and also a Roman Catholic school with 25 pupils. The number of pupils on the rolls of all the Government aided schools in 1921 was 1,717.
