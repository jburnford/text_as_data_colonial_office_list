Education.

The schools, which are wholly in the hands of the several denominations, receive grants-in-aid. There is one Anglican school, 1 Mohammadan, 3 Wesleyan, and 2 Roman Catholic, with about 1,400 scholars in all. Education is not compulsory, and fees are charged. In addition, the Wesleyan Society have established a Technical School on undenominational lines, to which the Government contribute 300£ a year; and there is also a Mohammadan School at which the ordinary rudiments of an English education are taught, the teachers of it being paid by the Government.
