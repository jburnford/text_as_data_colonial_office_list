Colony: Gambia
Year: 1965
Section: Development Plans
Type: narrative
---



Allocations totalling over £4 million have been made available from time to time under the Colonial Development and Welfare Acts 1945 to 1959.



The major works carried out under these Acts include:



(i) The Royal Victoria Hospital, Bathurst;

(ii) Bathurst town drainage and land reclamation schemes;

(iii) Bathurst new water supply;

(iv) Bitumenised roads in Bathurst;

(v) New High School, Bathurst;

(vi) Armitage School, Georgetown;

(vii) New primary and post-primary schools;

(viii) New health centres and rural dispensaries;

(ix) New veterinary headquarters;

(x) New staff quarters;

(xi) Oyster Creek Bridge, length 720 ft.;

(xii) Ferry terminals at Bathurst and Barra;

(xiii) Two of the three sections of the Southern Trunk Road.



Other schemes include research into pest infestation and agronomic problems; agricultural, forestry and veterinary improvements; the rice farm at Sapu; sociological and medical research and reconstruction of roads up country.



A V.H.F. Radio Telephone and Telegraph System was installed between 1961 and 1963. The runway of Yundum airport was completely reconstructed in 1963 under a free gift from Great Britain.



A programme of further development schemes was published during 1964. It covers a period of 3½ years from 1st January 1964 to 30th June 1967 and totalled £4,400,000.
