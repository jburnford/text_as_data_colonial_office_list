### Currency and Banking.

The currency is British West African silver coinage, British sterling, French 5 franc pieces (Government value, 3s. 10½d., trade value, 4s.), and West African currency notes (2½s., 10s. and 2s.). The number of 5 franc pieces in circulation has diminished greatly since the outbreak of war. There are two Banks in Bathurst; the Bank of British West Africa and the Colonial Bank. The latter was opened in December, 1917.

The average rate of exchange for bills drawn on London during the year was one per cent.

A Government savings bank was established in 1886, and had on 31st Dec., 1917, 6,719/., deposited by 710 persons.
