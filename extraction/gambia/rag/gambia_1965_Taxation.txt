Taxation



The main source of tax revenue is the customs tariff, which is at present supplemented by a "purchase tax" of 4%.



Company income tax is at the rates of 45% (9/- in the £).



Personal income tax is levied either on total income or on "chargeable" income under the following schedules, tax being paid on whichever schedule yields the higher tax:



**"Total Income" Schedule (not applicable to non-residents)**



| From £151 to £200 | £1 0 0 |

|-------------------|--------|

| From £201 to £250 | £1 10 0 |

| From £251 to £300 | £2 0 0 |

| From £301 to £350 | £3 0 0 |

| Exceeding £350    | 3d. per £ |



**"Chargeable Income" Sliding Scale Schedule**



| Rate per £ |

|------------|

| Up to £400 | 0s. 6d. |

| Between £401 and £800 | 1s. 0d. |

| Between £801 and £1200 | 2s. 6d. |

| Between £1201 and £2300 | 5s. 0d. |

| Between £2301 and £10,300 | 10s. 0d. |

| Above £10,300 | 15s. 0d. |



Chargeable Incomes of single persons up to £200 and of married persons up to £350 are exempt under this Schedule and there are liberal allowances for dependants, life insurance, passages etc.



Local rates are payable to the Bathurst Town Council and to Area Councils in the rural areas.



There is a direct tax on inhabitants of the rural areas known as the District Rate, which accrues to the Area Treasuries. The rate at present varies from 40s. in the more rural villages to 55s. in the larger centres. It is based on the "yard" of not more than four houses with thatched roofs. Houses roofed with corrugated iron carry a higher tax in most areas. There are additional rates for lodgers in the "yard" and "strange farmers".
