Colony: Gambia
Year: 1953
Section: The chief imports
Type: narrative
---
The chief imports are machinery, metal manufactures, bags and sacks, soap, cement, edible oils, tobacco, cotton yarn, cotton piece goods, wheaten flour, artificial silk piece goods, lumber, spirits, rice, motor cars and lorries, sugar, apparel, kola-nuts and petroleum products.

The Gambia Oilseeds Marketing Board handles the export of all groundnuts and other oilseeds. Its aims are to ensure orderly marketing, to improve the quality of exports, and from its profits to finance schemes of research and development. There are no registered co-operative societies.
