Education is not compulsory. There are fourteen elementary poor schools subsidised by Government, and several private schools, the number of scholars on average attendance being 1,867 for the year ending 30th March, 1898. The Government aided schools are chiefly managed by the clergy of the different denominations, and small fees are charged.
