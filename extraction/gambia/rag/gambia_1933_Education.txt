Education.

There is an Education Department under the direction of an Administrative Officer, who is seconded for duty as Superintendent of Education. The Christian schools, which are wholly in the hands of the several denominations, receive grants-in-aid. Of ordinary elementary schools in Bathurst, there are one Anglican, two Wesleyan and two Roman Catholic, with 1,605 scholars in all. Education is not compulsory, and fees are charged. There are two Wesleyan High schools and two Roman Catholic Secondary Schools, two for boys and two for girls, all under European control. There is also a Mohammedan school with 248 pupils, at which the ordinary rudiments of an English and Arabic education are taught, the upkeep of the premises and the payment of the teachers being borne by the Government, the native Mohammedan community contributing a small fixed annual sum. At MacCarthy Island there is an elementary Wesleyan school with 47 pupils, and a Government Mohammedan school, started in January, 1927, with 67 pupils on the roll. There are also three Wesleyan vernacular schools with a total enrolment of 90 pupils. A total Government grant-in-aid of £4,549. was made to the elementary schools, and of £500. to the secondary schools, during 1931.
