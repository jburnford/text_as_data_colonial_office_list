Climate.

The climate of the Gambia during the dry season from the end of November to the middle of May is very pleasant and healthy. But during the wet season—June to October—the conditions are much the same as elsewhere on the West Coast of Africa, though the change from excessive dryness to the damp atmosphere so prevalent in
the Gulf of Guinea probably makes them more felt. In the interior from March to June it is intensely hot.

The lowest reading of the thermometer in the shade during 1908 was at Bathurst 59 degrees in December, the highest recorded temperature being 98 in April, but at McCarthy's Island the shade maximum was 112.

The rainfall during the year was 43'54 inches. The rainfall varies considerably; the average may, however, be taken to be 50 inches.
