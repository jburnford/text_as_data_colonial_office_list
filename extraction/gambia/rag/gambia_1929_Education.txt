### Education

The Christian schools, which are wholly in the hands of the several denominations, receive grants-in-aid. Of ordinary elementary schools in Bathurst, there are one Anglican, two Wesleyan and two Roman Catholic, with 1,531 scholars in all. Education is not compulsory, and fees are charged. There are two Wesleyan High schools, one for boys and one for girls, and St. Joseph's Convent School for girls, all under European control. There is also a Mohammedan school with 264 pupils, at which the ordinary rudiments of an English and Arabic education are taught, the upkeep of the premises and the payment of the teachers being borne by the Government, the native Mohammedan community contributing a small fixed annual sum. At MacCarthy Island...
there is an elementary Wesleyan school with 72 pupils, and a Government Mohammadan school, started in January, 1927, with 83 pupils on the roll. A total Government grant in aid of £1,447l. was made to the elementary schools, and of £300l. to the secondary schools, during 1927.
