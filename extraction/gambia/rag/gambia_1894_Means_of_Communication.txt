Means of Communication.

The Liverpool mail steamers call every fortnight, and there is also communication via Dakar with Bordeaux. Four telegraph cables place Bathurst in communication with Europe and West Africa. There are no railways, internal telegraphs, or metalled roads, save one from Bathurst to Cape St. Mary 7½ miles across the Oyster Creek Bridge, which connects the island of St. Mary with the mainland of British Combo. Horses will live, and the river forms an unrivalled highway for 250 miles. The postage of letters to the United Kingdom is 2½d. per ¼ oz., to the rest of Europe, United States, and West Africa, 2½d. per ½ oz. There is no internal post.
