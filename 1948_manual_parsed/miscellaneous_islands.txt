MISCELLANEOUS ISLANDS

(Not included in the main portion of the historical and statistical account above.)

A number of islands and rocks throughout the world are British territory or under British protection but are not included in any colony or separate protectorate. Many of these have no permanent inhabitants. Examples are the Ashmore Group (Indian Ocean), Bird Island and Cato Island (in the Norfolk Island group), Prince Island and Bell Cay (near British New Guinea), Caroline Island, Flint Island and Vostoc Island (Pacific Ocean 9° 56' S. latitude, 150° 6' W. longitude and 11° 26' S. latitude and 151° 48' W. longitude), Malden Island (4° 1' S. latitude, 155° 57' W. longitude) and Starbuck Island; and there are many others.
