ADMINISTRATION
The City Council’s receipts for 1950 were £396,724, its expenditure £378,129 and its debt on the 31st December, 1950, £322,653.

SOCIAL SERVICES

Education
Government expenditure on education in 1950 amounted to £48,508 whilst estimated expenditure in 1951 was £56,500.

Labour and Welfare
The Accidents and Occupational Diseases (Notification) Ordinance, No. 21 of 1950, which was brought into force on the 27th December, 1950, requires both private and official employers to notify to the Director of Labour and Welfare any accidents or prescribed occupational diseases arising out of and in the course of employment of any worker which cause loss of life or disablement for more than three days.

The continued rise in the cost of living has been accompanied by increases in the cost of living allowances paid to industrial workers.

PUBLIC REVENUE AND EXPENDITURE
(See Note on page 27)

In 1950 the Revenue of the Colony amounted to £820,557 and the Expenditure to £937,060 (excluding £254,009 expended on Public Housing financed from Loan Funds). The Public Debt amounted to £1,400,000. No grants-in-aid of administration were made during the year, but £35,692 was received as a grant under Colonial Development and Welfare Acts.

CURRENCY AND BANKING
Colonial Government currency notes in circulation at 31st December, 1950, amounted to £916,515.
