SOCIAL SERVICES

Health

The Central Medical School in Suva now has a teaching staff of 3 whole time officers and 14 honorary lecturers. The whole time officers consist of the principal, assistant principal and a dental officer. The honorary lecturers include the medical and surgical specialists and other medical officers of the department. The school was founded in 1884 for the training of Fiji youths. Since 1928, however, its benefits have been available to other Western Pacific territories and its graduates, now numbering over 300, include Fijians, Fiji Indians, Western Samoans, Eastern Samoans, Tongans, Gilbertese, Ellice Islanders, Solomon Islanders, New Hebrideans, Rotumans, Nauruans, Cook Islanders and Nieué Islanders. In January, 1951, 29 dental students and 37 medical students from the U.S. Trust Territory, Pacific Islands, who had been enrolled at the U.S. Naval Medical School at Guam were transferred to the Central Medical School in Suva. A larger central medical school and hostel to accommodate 150 students is being built.

Postal and Telegraphs

Overseas surface mails to New Zealand and United Kingdom via New Zealand are despatched at an average of three times each month and similarly to Australia and India, via Australia Mails to North and South America and United Kingdom via Canada are despatched approximately twice monthly. Airmails to United Kingdom, Near and Far East and Australia are despatched five times weekly and to New Zealand, North and South America and Hawaii, thrice weekly. Samoa is served weekly and the Cook Islands fortnightly.

Internal radio stations have been established at the following places:—Suva, Lautoka, Nayavu, Levuka, Moturiki, Makogai, Nukuse, Lambasai, Savusavu, Nambouwalu, Mbutha Bay, Rambi Island, Waiyovo (Taveuni), Ndreketi, Natewa, Wainunu, Lomaloma, Rotuma, Lakeba, Matuku Island, Kambara Island, Mbenga Island, Mothe Island, Fulanga Island, Oneata Island, Koro Island and Ngau Island (fixed stations owned and operated by the Fiji Government): M.V. "Andi Maopa", M.V. "Viti II", M.V. "Dengei" (Fiji Government ships): Nandi Airport, Undu Point, Ono-I-Lau, Yasawa-I-Rara (N.Z. Meteorological Stations): Wakaya Island, Katafanga Island, Mambavatu and Naitamba (privately-owned stations). Services are confined to circuits between Suva and other Government and private stations for public correspondence and Nandi Airport and meteorological stations for weather traffic, except in emergency. The following Fiji Government radio-telephone stations were to be in operation by the end of 1951: Moala Island, Totoya Island, Vatoa Island, Nayau Island, Vanua Vatu Island, Namuka-I-Lau Island, Waimotu (Vanua Levu) and Vunindawa (Viti Levu).
