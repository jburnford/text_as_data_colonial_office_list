**ZANZIBAR**

See pages 434–439 of *The Colonial Office List 1951*.

---

**Public Finance**

(See Note on page 27.)

In 1950 the Revenue amounted to £1,469,835 and the Expenditure to £1,028,088.

---

**Revenue**

The chief sources of revenue in 1950 were:

- Import Duty, £446,383; Clove Duty, £488,448; Licences, etc., £118,724; and Colonial Development and Welfare Grants, £159,983.

**Expenditure**

The chief heads of expenditure in 1950 were:

- Agriculture, £91,378; Health, £110,197; Education, £163,862; Provincial Administration, £52,333; Public Works, £237,698; and Pensions, £110,757.

**Taxation**

The principal sources of taxation are:

- Customs Import Duties (£446,383 in 1950); Clove Duty (£488,448 in 1950); and Income Tax (£78,792 in 1950).

**Currency and Banking**

Money in circulation on the 31st December, 1950, amounted to:

- Notes, £441,591; Coin, £362,674.

The number of depositors of the Government Savings Bank at the end of 1950 was 16,321 and the total amount of deposits, £450,460.

**Production and Trade**

(See Note on page 27.)

Export of cloves in 1950 amounted to 353,960 cwts. valued at £3,199,945. The value of clove oil exported during the year was £113,621 (343,565 lbs.) as compared with £77,058 (320,882 lbs.) in 1949.

During 1950 all copra was exported in the form of coconut oil, amounting to 8,009 tons and valued at £786,086. The value of other coconut products, including soap and oil cake, amounted to £157,468.
