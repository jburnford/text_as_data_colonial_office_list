BRUNEI

See pages 107–110 of The Colonial Office List 1951.

CONSTITUTION

The Council now consists of 13 members, including the British Resident, with the Sultan as President.

ADMINISTRATION

The phrase “... which has control over the districts of Belait and Tutong” appearing at the end of the first paragraph under this heading on page 107 of The Colonial Office List 1951 should be disregarded.

SOCIAL SERVICES

Education

There are now 28 Malay vernacular schools in the State. The number of boys attending these vernacular schools is now 2,255 and the number of girls, 427.

The number of Chinese vernacular schools has increased to six.

COMMUNICATIONS

The mail service between Labuan and Brunei is now operated thrice-weekly by the Brunei Government.

The air mail service to and from Singapore via Labuan carrying first-class mail (letters and postcards) is now also operated thrice-weekly.

PUBLIC REVENUE AND EXPENDITURE

(See Note on page 27.)

In 1950 the Revenue totalled $17,302,862 and the Expenditure $7,112,499. The State has no Public Debt. No grants-in-aid of administration or grants and loans under Colonial Development and Welfare Acts were made during the year.

PRODUCTION AND TRADE

(See Note on page 27.)

Main Products

The yield of unmilled rice in 1950 was 2,587,529 gallons.

Exports

In 1950, 4,011,463 tons of mineral oils were exported and 2,558 tons of rubber. The total value of all exports for the year, most of which were to Singapore, was $205,388,521.

Imports

Imports into the State in 1950 included:

The total value of all imports for the year, most of which were supplied from Singapore, was $60,265,034.
