POPULATION

The death rate in 1950 was 10·06 per 1,000 and the birth rate, 30·07.

SOCIAL SERVICES

Education

There is now only one Government primary school in the Colony in addition to the six Church of England and twenty Roman Catholic aided primary schools and the small private schools.

The enrolment in all schools in the Colony in December, 1950, was 2,315 boys and 2,441 girls.

Government expenditure on education in 1950 was Rs. 287,933 from local funds and Rs. 101,979 from the Colonial Development and Welfare Fund.

Information Services

Programmes of talks, news and music are now broadcast only on Sundays from ZCQ 3, on a frequency of 5,770 Kc/s. and a wavelength of 52 metres.

Health

The Medical Department controls one well-equipped hospital at Victoria, and small, but adequately equipped, hospitals at Anse Royale, Praslin Island and La Digue Island. There is a mental hospital at Anse Royale and a settlement for the treatment of leprosy on Curieuse Island. Dispensaries at four other centres are held weekly.

There is also a quarantine station at Long Island and a home for the aged in Victoria.

A well-equipped bacteriological laboratory now exists adjacent to Victoria Hospital.

Intestinal disease forms the main health problem. There is no malaria, the anopheles mosquito being non-existent in the Seychelles.

The estimated expenditure on Medical and Health Services in 1950 was Rs. 421,865.
Labour and Welfare

During the year 1950 a sum of Rs. 32,206 was spent on outdoor relief for 854 persons. The average population of the Fiennes Institute was 69 for the year maintained on a cost of Rs. 11,800, excluding salaries.

The revision of the poor relief records is now almost complete.

PUBLIC FINANCE

(See Note on page 27.)

In 1950 the estimated Revenue totalled Rs. 3,976,990 and the estimated Expenditure, Rs. 3,693,751. The Colony's Public Debt amounted to Rs. 390,216. No grants-in-aid of Administration were received during the year but an amount estimated at Rs. 423,148 was received under the Colonial Development and Welfare Acts.

The surplus of assets over liabilities on the 31st December, 1950, was Rs. 4,393,898. The General Revenue Stabilization Reserve stood at Rs. 1,592,126.

Duty on flour, maize and rice has been abolished.

Income Tax

Income Tax is chargeable on all net income remaining after the following deductions unless the chargeable income is less than Rs. 500, in which case no tax is payable:

Earned Income relief 1/6th of earned income. (Maximum Rs.1,500)
Resident's allowance Rs. 500 (or Rs. 2,000 for a married man living with or wholly maintaining his wife)

Child allowance Rs. 500 per child. (Maximum Rs.2,000)
Dependent relative Rs. 500.
Life insurance Amount of premiums. (Maximum 1/4th of total income)

The rates of tax are on a sliding scale commencing at 2½ per cent. on the first Rs. 3,000 of chargeable income and 5 per cent. on chargeable income between Rs. 3,000 and Rs. 6,000. Thereafter the rates of tax increase by 2½ per cent. for each Rs. 1,000 of chargeable income up to a rate of 27½ per cent. for chargeable income between Rs.14,000 and Rs. 15,000. For chargeable income between Rs.15,000 and Rs.25,000 the rate is 30 per cent. and between Rs. 25,000 and Rs. 40,000, 40 per cent. Over Rs. 40,000 the rate is 50 per cent.
