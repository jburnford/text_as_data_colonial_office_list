THE GAMBIA

See pages 137–144 of The Colonial Office List 1951.

SITUATION AND AREA

The total area of the Colony, excluding the parts administered as a Protectorate, is 29·4 square miles (not 69 square miles as stated in The Colonial Office List 1951).

The area of the Protectorate, including the parts of the Colony administered as a Protectorate, is 3,974 square miles (not 3,964 square miles).

CONSTITUTION

The Executive Council was revised during 1951 and, at the end of the year, consisted of the Governor as President, the Colonial Secretary (ex-Officio), and five official and four unofficial members all of whom were nominated.

An amending Legislative Council Order-in-Council was made on the 30th June, 1951, and the changes are reflected in the new composition of the Council given below.

ADMINISTRATION

(b) Protectorate

The total revenue from the group and district treasuries established under the Protectorate Treasuries Ordinance, 1945, was £60,427 in 1950–51.

COMMUNICATIONS

The airfield is at Yundum, distant 17 miles from Bathurst. It is controlled by the Government and operated on behalf of the Ministry of Civil Aviation by International Aeradio Ltd. The Public Works Department maintains runways, buildings, power and water supplies. The Meteorological Service is operated by the West African Meteorological Service. The regular scheduled service calling at Yundum is that of the West African Airways Corporation which calls twice weekly on the route Lagos–Accra–Roberts Field–Freetown–Dakar. This service connects northwards at Dakar with the trunk routes between the United Kingdom, Europe and South America and southwards via Sierra Leone, Gold Coast and Nigeria, with South Africa. Yundum is also used as a major diversion airport for Dakar by the British Overseas Airways Corporation and Air France.

PUBLIC FINANCE

(See Note on page 27.)

In 1950 the Revenue totalled £999,217 and the Expenditure, £1,062,312. The Public Debt amounted to £38,760 in respect of which the Sinking Fund stood at £18,354.

CURRENCY AND BANKING

On the 31st December, 1950, deposits in the Government Savings Bank amounted to £179,537, the number of depositors being 6,245.

IMPORTS AND EXPORTS

(See Note on page 27.)

(Fuller particulars are to be found in the Annual Customs Reports.) The total imports of the Gambia in 1950 were valued at £2,921,008, of which £1,749,247 were imported from the United Kingdom, £481,082 from other British countries and £690,679 from elsewhere.

The total value of the territory’s exports during the year was £2,160,483, of which £2,159,512 were exported to the United Kingdom, £688 to other British countries, and £283 to elsewhere.
