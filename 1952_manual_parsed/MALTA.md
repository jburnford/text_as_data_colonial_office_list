MALTA

See pages 255–263 of The Colonial Office List 1951.

GENERAL DESCRIPTION

In December, 1950 there were 72 vessels registered at Malta, the gross tonnage being 19,838.

SOCIAL SERVICES

Education

In 1950 there were 111 primary schools with 43,613 pupils.

The number of literacy classes fell to 82 during the year, the attendance being 1,200.

Public Works

The following smaller towns and villages have an efficient system of sewage disposal: Cottonera, Sliema, St. Julians, Msida, Pietà, Kalkara, Zabbar, Hamrun, Qormi, Balzan, Lija, Attard, Birkirkara, Mdina, Rabat, Tarxien, Pawla, Zejtun, Ghaxaq and Luqa.

During the financial year 1950–51, interceptors from Zebbug to Siggiewi, and Ghaxaq to Gudja were continued, and delivery mains started from the proposed pumping stations at Birzebbuqa and Dingli to Ghaxaq and Rabat respectively.

Street sewers have been laid in the villages of Mellieha, Zebbug, Mosta, Naxxar, and new sections in Hamrun and Birkirkara. Small extensions have been carried out in Qormi, Rabat, Pawla, Tarxien, Zabbar, Gzira, Pietà and St. Julians.

With regard to the general scheme for the collection and disposal of domestic refuse, two consignments of the necessary machinery were delivered during 1950 and the delivery of the remaining consignment is expected by the end of 1951.

Xaghra should be added to the list of villages in Gozo supplied with electric current.

COMMUNICATIONS

There are now 4,724 telephone exchange lines with 7,231 stations.
FINANCE

(See Note on page 27.)

In 1950-51 the total Revenue amounted to £5,720,191 and the Expenditure to £5,802,106. Grants and loans under the Colonial Development and Welfare Acts, received during the year, amounted to £76,901.

BANKING AND CURRENCY

The amount of local currency in circulation on the 31st March, 1951, was £15,497,882 10s. 0d.

Barclays Bank (D.C. & O.) now has six branches in Malta.

On the 31st March, 1951, deposits in the Government Savings Bank amounted to £17,228,286, with 54,113 depositors.

TRADE

Customs Tariff

The amount of duty collected in 1950 from specific duties was £1,274,559, and from ad valorem duties £1,081,805.

IMPORTS

(See Note on page 27.)

The total value of imports in 1950 was £16,687,132, of which imports from the United Kingdom were valued at £7,032,661, from other British countries £3,167,951, and from elsewhere £6,486,520. In addition the value of bullion and specie imported amounted to £72,700.

EXPORTS (including Re-exports)

(See Note on page 27.)

In 1950, total exports were valued at £1,660,759. Exports to the United Kingdom were valued at £675,867, to other British countries £122,952, and to elsewhere £861,940. The export of bullion and specie was valued at £450.

The value of re-exports to the United Kingdom in 1950 was £284,806; to other parts of the British Empire £43,689; to foreign countries £163,901; and as ships' stores, etc., £349,944.

SHIPPING ENTERED AND CLEARED

In 1950 the total tonnage of shipping entered was 1,985,617, and cleared 1,987,194, of which British tonnage amounted to 928,161, and 929,738 respectively.

Total Weight of Goods Transhipped. 1950—1,106 tons.
