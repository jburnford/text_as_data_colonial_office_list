BERMUDA

See pages 81–87 of The Colonial Office List 1951.

POPULATION

The total population, excluding service personnel, was estimated at 37,394 as a result of census returns at October, 1950. The figure for the white population was 13,497 and for the coloured, 23,897.

CONSTITUTION

The members of the Executive Council and the Legislature are now paid at the rate of 24s. for each day’s attendance.

SOCIAL SERVICES

Education

The Board of Education now consists of a Chairman and ten members.

The present value of the Rhodes Scholarship is £368 per annum.

Health

More than half the adult population has had a chest X-ray. The incidence of active tuberculosis is about 1 in 1,500 in both the white and coloured communities and is falling satisfactorily.

V.D. has been under compulsory treatment since 1943 with good results and, with penicillin treatment, compulsion is seldom needed.

The King Edward VII Hospital (general) received a subsidy of £85,000 in 1950, the Nursing Home £6,000 and the Bermuda Welfare Society, £2,900.

The Department of Health maintain a school service and has planned parenthood, pre-natal and pre-school age clinics. Food-handlers are examined. Milk is pasteurised.

Mosquito control has been in force since 1942 with the practical elimination of *aedes egypti* but *culex fatigans* still maintains strongholds.

Rat control has been started.

The infant mortality rate is 31·3 for the white and 45·2 for the coloured population per 1,000 births. The general death rate is 8·5 and 8·9 respectively per 1,000.

Labour

The “Teachers’ Association of Bermuda” (26 members) has now been registered as a trade union. The membership of the other teachers’ union has increased from 114 to 121.

COMMUNICATIONS

Roads

No person may use a motor car which at the time of its importation was a used car.

Air

British Overseas Airways Corporation, Pan American World Airways and Colonial Airlines maintain regular shuttle services between Bermuda and the United States of America. British Overseas Airways Corporation also operate a twice-weekly service from London through Bermuda to Nassau, Jamaica and return. Trans-Canada Airlines operate a shuttle service weekly to Bermuda from Montreal and Toronto, extending one flight per week through to Barbados and Trinidad. Aerovias Guest, S.A., stop at Bermuda on their regular weekly service each way between Mexico City and Europe via Miami and the Azores. Iberia Airlines and Compania Cubanas also operate a weekly service across the mid-Atlantic route via Bermuda between Madrid and Havana, Cuba. Avianca flies twice a month east-bound only through Bermuda and the Azores to Europe, from Baranquilla, returning via the South Atlantic.

Cable and Wireless (West Indies) Limited

Direct radio telephone circuits are now operated with New York, Barbados and London and through these distant terminals long distance facilities are available with U.S.A., Canada, the United Kingdom, the British West Indies, and many other parts of the world.

Radio telephone services are now available with the T.S.S. “Ocean Monarch”.

The aeronautical point-to-point services provided by the Company under contract with the Air Ministry and the Bermuda Government no longer cover Prestwick.

PUBLIC REVENUE AND EXPENDITURE
