SOCIAL SERVICES

COMMUNICATIONS

Air

The air services listed under this sub-heading in The Colonial Office List 1951 continue to operate with the exception of Servicio Aereo de Honduras S.A. (SASHA). The BWIA service to Jamaica, however, now operates only once weekly.

In addition, Caribbean International Airways (CIA) maintain a bi-weekly service from and to Grand Cayman, B.W.I.; and a TACA International Airways service covers the route New Orleans–Belize–Guatemala and the return flight once a week.

Telecommunications facilities are provided for aviation services at Stanley Field.

Posts and Telegraphs

Telephone offices which also transact telegraph business have increased to 133.

As regards external communications maintained by wireless, the Tropical Radio Telegraph Company’s stations concerned are now at Miami and Tegucigalpa.

Items of news interest and forecast for the Western Caribbean are broadcast daily at 12.30 p.m. and 7.15 p.m. from the Belize Broadcasting Station “Radio Belize”, on a frequency of 4,950 Kilocycles. Storm and hurricane warnings are broadcast during the hurricane season from June to November.
